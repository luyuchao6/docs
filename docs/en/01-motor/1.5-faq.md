# 1.5 FAQ

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What are the baud rate and sample point settings for the motor's CAN FD communication?**

    Answer: Arbitration segment 1 Mbps, data segment 5 Mbps. Sample point is 75%.

2. **What signal type does CAN use?**

    Answer: CAN uses differential signals. CAN_H is the high-level signal line and CAN_L is the low-level signal line.

3. **Individual control works, but controlling multiple motors simultaneously does not.**

    Answer: This is most likely caused by the power supply's current limit being set too low. When multiple motors start simultaneously, the instantaneous current is too high, causing the motors to report an error.

4. **Does the motor have a torque sensor?**

    Answer: No, force control is implemented using the motor's own current loop.

1. **Does the protocol support CAN 2.0A or CAN 2.0B?**

    Answer: CAN 2.0B.

1. **How do you measure the termination resistance?**

    Answer: Measure the resistance with power off. A resistance of around 60 Ω on a CAN bus is normal. Measuring while the program is running yields meaningless results.

2. **Under what circumstances is a 120 Ω resistor needed?**

    Answer: It is recommended to connect 120 Ω termination resistors at both ends of the CAN bus to reduce interference on the CAN bus.

3. **What power supply is needed for a 6-motor series robotic arm?**

    Answer: A DC regulated power supply is recommended. For the voltage operating range, refer to the product manual. Set the current limit to at least 10 A to prevent errors caused by excessive inrush current when the motors start.

4. **What is the equivalent moment of inertia at the motor shaft for the 4438 joint?**

    Answer: The moment of inertia at the motor shaft is 2.9355×10^-8 kg·m².

1. **Single-turn position retention after motor power-off**

    Answer: The motor uses a single-turn absolute encoder. After power is cycled, the motor position only records the single-turn position, in the range of -0.5 to +0.5 turns.

    For example: if the motor is rotated to 1.3 turns and then power-cycled, the recorded position is 0.3 turns. If rotated to 0.8 turns and then power-cycled, the recorded position is -0.2 turns.
